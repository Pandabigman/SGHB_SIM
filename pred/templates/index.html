üî¨ Model Overview

This simulator uses real microsatellite genetic data from 199 wild Southern Ground Hornbills (4 populations in South Africa) and 145 captive individuals (from PAAZA, AZA, and EAZA zoos) to predict population viability under different conservation scenarios over the next 50-100 generations (~1,300-2,600 years).

üéØ Conservation Scenarios Explained

Model 1: Baseline

All 4 wild populations maintained (Eastern Cape, Kruger, KwaZulu-Natal, Limpopo). No supplementation.

Model 2: Population Loss

Simulates loss of Eastern Cape + KwaZulu-Natal populations. Shows consequences of reduced connectivity.

Model 3: Low Supplementation

4 PAAZA (South African zoo) birds released per generation. Moderate genetic rescue effect.

Model 4: High Supplementation

10 PAAZA birds per generation. Strong genetic rescue with substantial demographic boost.

Model 5: International Mix

4 birds from mixed sources (PAAZA + AZA + EAZA). Maximizes novel allele introduction.
